The Sitting Tree

Founded by Brandon Stuart, Erik Lima and Vince Villanueva, The Sitting Tree's goal is straight forward: lend a helping hand to disadvantaged families in need. Whatever the ailment may be, we acknowledge that when an economic, medical, or other form of hardship affects a specific individual, their family members suffer along their side.

Our organization identifies ways to help these families as a whole, and allows, even if only temporarily, a feeling of empowerment under strain. Our projects include giving out groceries for meals during holiday seasons, providing assistance with transportation costs to hospital visits, and donating school supplies to underprivileged families.

"The Sitting Tree" is an actual place where one of our founders would go to think. A fallen tree offered the perfect seat for reflection and temporary relief. This idea is embodied through The Sitting Tree as we lend a helping hand in scenarios where one is greatly needed.

Helping families re-connect with hope

During my time working as a family therapist in a children's hospital, I saw how meaningful "simple" things like complimentary meals and hotel stays were in taking pressure off of family members, not to mention what fun activities could do in helping kids be carefree and not feel like their diagnoses defined them. By easing these families' burdens, they are helping them re-connect with hope. I believe that when people have hope, they have the capacity to withstand even the greatest of challenges. I look forward to seeing how the caring reach of this organization spreads and touches even more lives for good.
